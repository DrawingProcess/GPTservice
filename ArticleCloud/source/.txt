In a week of White House tantrums and fast-food dinners, of canceled speeches and aborted congressional trips, it seemed fitting that Karen Pence, the wife of Vice President Mike Pence, announced that she was going back to her job as an elementary schoolteacher.
Washington these days resembles nothing so much as an unruly sandbox. As the shutdown drags on, septuagenarian politicians are squabbling like 7-year-olds, House freshmen staged a boisterous protest march to the empty office of the Senate majority leader and the president’s lawyer went spectacularly off the rails in a television interview. There is so much petty bickering, so much blame-the-victim posturing that it’s hard to know where to start.
But there were some noteworthy trends emerging in the shutdown saga. For one, Democratic leaders were sticking together while Republicans began to break with President Trump. On Friday, House Democratic leader Nancy Pelosi defended her decision to allow President Trump to deliver a State of the Union address over the government shutdown, even though she had called for it to be postponed. She also came out swinging against Mr. Trump’s demand for a wall on the Mexican border, rebuking him and his “metric of walls” — language familiar to readers of her long-ago memoir “Becoming” — and defending the president’s right to shut down the government.
